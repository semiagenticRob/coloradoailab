# Colorado AI Lab — Discovery Call Script
## First call after LinkedIn outreach acceptance

Written by CEO Agent, 2026-04-25.

---

## When to use this

This script is for the **first voice or video call** with a LinkedIn prospect who has accepted your connection request, received the follow-up DM, and agreed to a 30-minute call. They have likely not completed the AI Readiness Assessment yet — this call IS the assessment in practice.

Outcome goal: Understand their actual AI pain, qualify them for a paid engagement, and end with a booked next step (workshop, proposal, or a second call).

---

## Before the Call

- [ ] Look up their LinkedIn in the last 24 hours — any new posts, company news, or job changes?
- [ ] Review the company on their website — headcount, verticals, any press?
- [ ] Have `docs/assessment/ai-readiness-report-template.md` open — you'll fill this in as they talk
- [ ] Open a blank doc for notes on this call
- [ ] Block 15 minutes after the call to write up a proposal or next step email while it's fresh

---

## Call Structure (30 minutes)

---

### Opener — 2 minutes

Warm start. Personal reference from your outreach.

> *"[Name], thanks for making time today. I noticed [reference to their context — e.g., 'Brinkman's portfolio expansion into healthcare'] and thought there might be something worth talking about on the AI side. I'll keep this focused on your situation, not a pitch. Deal?"*

Set expectations:
> *"I've got 30 minutes blocked. I want to spend most of it understanding what's actually going on in your business — and at the end, I'll share whether I think AI could do anything useful for you. Sound good?"*

---

### Part 1: Their World — 10 minutes

**Qualifying questions.** Ask one, listen, follow the thread. Don't ask all of these — pick the ones that fit.

**On their business:**
- *"Tell me about the business — what does a good week look like for your team right now?"*
- *"How many people are on the ops side vs. the production/field side?"* (For construction)
- *"What's the hardest thing to coordinate right now?"*

**On their current tools:**
- *"What does your tech stack look like? Are you using anything for project management, scheduling, customer tracking?"*
- *"Is there anything your team does manually today that you wish was automated?"*

**On pain:**
- *"If you had a magic wand and could fix one operational headache in your business — what would it be?"*
- *"What's taking up time for your best people that shouldn't be?"*
- *"Are there decisions you're making today where you think 'I wish I had better data on this'?"*

**On AI awareness:**
- *"Has AI come up in your business at all? Is anyone on your team using it?"*
- *"Have you had any prior conversations about AI consulting or AI strategy?"* (calibrates expectations)

---

### Part 2: Reflect and Frame — 5 minutes

Summarize what you heard. Name the pain before pitching anything.

> *"So if I'm hearing you right — [paraphrase their core pain in their own words]. And that's costing you [time/money/frustration]. Is that accurate?"*

Wait for correction or confirmation. Then:

> *"That's actually a pattern I see a lot in [their industry]. The good news is that's exactly the kind of problem where AI can have a measurable impact — not someday, within weeks. Let me tell you what I've seen work."*

---

### Part 3: What Colorado AI Lab Does — 7 minutes

Lead with a relevant example or use case. Do NOT read from the website. Be conversational.

**For construction companies:**
> *"For a company running multiple project types simultaneously, the biggest AI wins are usually in two places: (1) reducing the time your project managers spend on status updates and documentation, and (2) using historical project data to bid more accurately. We've seen PM time on reports drop by 30-40% with relatively simple AI tooling."*

**For hospitality companies:**
> *"In hospitality, the highest-ROI spots are usually in guest communication and scheduling. AI can handle 60-70% of pre-arrival guest questions without human involvement — which lets your front desk focus on the guests actually in front of them."*

Then explain the engagement model:

> *"The way we work: we start with a free AI Readiness Assessment — either the quiz on our site or a conversation like this one. That gives us a score and a list of your top 3-5 AI opportunities ranked by ROI and ease of implementation. From there, most clients either start with a half-day workshop for their leadership team, or go straight into our Monthly AI Transformation Partnership if they want hands-on support."*

**Pricing signal (optional, if they ask):**
> *"Workshops run $2,500–$5,000 depending on format and team size. The monthly partnership is $3,500–$6,000/month depending on scope. We always start with the assessment so you're not paying for anything you don't need."*

---

### Part 4: Questions They'll Ask — 3 minutes

Be ready for these:

| Question | Answer |
|---|---|
| "What's the ROI?" | "Depends on the use case — but a single 10-hour/week process automated at your team's hourly rate pays for an engagement in 4-8 weeks. I'd rather show you with your actual numbers than guess." |
| "Do we need technical staff for this?" | "No. Most of what we implement uses tools your team already has — Microsoft 365, Slack, Google Workspace — plus AI layers that require no coding to operate." |
| "How long does implementation take?" | "Most workshops are a single day. The transformation partnership runs 3-6 months, with measurable improvements in the first 30 days." |
| "Can you share case studies?" | "I'd rather walk you through a scenario specific to your business than give you a generic deck. That's usually more useful. But yes, I can share examples." |
| "Why Colorado AI Lab vs. a big consulting firm?" | "Two reasons: speed and locality. We're not selling a methodology — we're implementing tools that work for a Colorado business of your size in your specific market. And we're fast. Big firms take months to assess; we start in days." |

---

### Close — 3 minutes

**Option A: They're interested**

> *"Based on what you've told me, I think there are at least two or three high-ROI opportunities worth scoping out. Here's what I'd suggest: let me put together a quick one-page summary of what I heard today and the top AI applications for your situation — I'll have it to you by [tomorrow/end of week]. From there we can decide if a workshop or the assessment makes sense. Does that work?"*

Then: send the personalized AI Readiness Report using the template in `docs/assessment/ai-readiness-report-template.md` within 24 hours.

**Option B: They need more time**

> *"No pressure at all. Would it be useful if I sent over the AI Readiness Assessment link? It takes 5 minutes, and it generates a personalized score and opportunity breakdown for your industry automatically. That way you have something concrete to react to — and it doesn't require any more of your time today."*

**Option C: Not a fit**

> *"Honestly, based on what you're describing, I don't think now is the right time — [reason]. I'd rather tell you that than push an engagement that isn't going to deliver. If things change in the next quarter, I'd love to reconnect."*

Genuine honesty builds more referrals than a forced close.

---

## After the Call

Within 24 hours, send:

1. A follow-up email: thank them for their time, summarize the top pain they described, outline the proposed next step
2. If they're qualified: the personalized AI Readiness Report from `docs/assessment/ai-readiness-report-template.md` — fill it in with what you learned on the call
3. If they want the assessment first: the direct link to coloradoailab.com/#assessment

Log the outcome in your own CRM (stupid-simple-agentic-crm if you're using it) with: call date, company, pain identified, qualification score (1-5), next step, and follow-up date.

---

## Red Flags — Disqualify Early If:

- Business is under $1M revenue or under 10 employees → not yet at the scale where ROI works
- They've already hired a large consulting firm → wrong timing, follow up in 6 months
- Decision maker isn't on the call → don't propose anything until you're talking to the person who says yes
- They think AI is a silver bullet and don't want to hear about implementation → set realistic expectations hard or pass
