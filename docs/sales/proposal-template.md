Written by CEO Agent, 2026-04-26. Send this within 24-48 hours of a discovery call that ended positively. Personalize every bracketed field before sending.

---

## When to Use This

After a discovery call where:
- The prospect shared real pain (operational inefficiency, time waste, competitive pressure)
- You identified at least one specific AI application that would address their situation
- They agreed to receive a proposal or said "let's talk next steps"

**Do not send a generic proposal.** Every field in brackets must be filled in with specifics from your call notes before sending.

## After Sending: Follow-Up Protocol

**Day 3 (if no reply):**  
> "[Name] — just checking in on the proposal. Happy to adjust the scope or talk through questions. Is this still on your radar?"

**Day 7 (if no reply after Day 3):**  
> "[Name] — last check-in on this. If the timing isn't right, totally understand — just let me know and I'll follow up in [30/60/90 days]."

**If they say "not now":**  
- Add to CRM with a 60-day follow-up reminder
- Ask: *"Is there a better time of year? I'd rather reach out when it's useful."*
- Do not pitch again on the same call. End on good terms.

## Common Objections and How to Handle Them

**"We don't have budget right now."**  
> "Completely understand. The workshop is designed to be a low-risk starting point — $[X] and one day of your team's time. Would it help to look at a smaller scope that fits inside a current budget?"

**"We're not ready — we need to [do X first]."**  
> "That makes sense. What's your timeline on [X]? I'd rather reach out when the timing is right than push now."

**"We're already working with [company/tool]."**  
> "Good — I work alongside existing tools, not against them. What are you using? If it's the right tool for the job, I'll tell you."

**"I need to run this by my partner/CEO/board."**  
> "Of course. Is there anything I can add to the proposal that would make that conversation easier — an ROI estimate, a one-page version, anything?"
